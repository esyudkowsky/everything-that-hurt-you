# voice-script.md — emotional direction for voice acting

**What this is.** Every line of dialogue in `script.txt` (see the comment-free copy,
`claude-notes/script-nocomments.txt`), annotated with my (Fable's) best guess at what
the character was *actually feeling* when they said it, and — separately, where the two
diverge — *what they were trying to outwardly present as feeling*. This is an experiment
(author, 2026-07-08): direction by inner state rather than by prosody. We will later, not
immediately, test whether this works better for voice acting than explicit instructions
about intonation or delivery.

**How to use it.** Act the *feeling*. Where a *presenting* line exists, the character is
doing their own acting — perform the inner state, then let the character's attempted
performance sit on top of it, imperfectly. The audible gap between the two IS the scene.
Where no *presenting* is given, the character isn't masking; what they feel is what shows.

**Conventions.**
- `|` inside a quoted line is a beat/pause already in the script text (the engine renders
  it as a typing pause). It is not punctuation to be read aloud.
- Lines whose entire text is `...` are silent panels — nothing is spoken. They're included
  (marked *silent*) because the inner state may be worth a breath, and the actor should
  know what's happening in the silence either side of their lines.
- Physical/vocal state words ("tense", "loose", "throat tight") are offered where they
  seem load-bearing; treat them as body notes, not prosody notes.
- Third-person narration lines ("Avram slides coins across the counter.") and on-screen
  text (`@overlay` blocks, the pamphlet title) are NOT dialogue and are excluded. If
  narration is ever voiced, it is Skagganauk's voice — dry, vast, unhurried — same
  register as his first-person lines below.

**Spoilers are required reading for the cast.** The performances only work if the actors
know everything, because both leads spend the whole story concealing things the audience
learns late.

## Cast — inner-life notes (anchor everything to these)

**AVRAM DETWILER.** Died on Earth (truck), summoned to Elhom IV, and — crucially — he
*knows the genre*. He is the Summoned Hero and tells no one until the last scene. He
believes the nobles quietly arrange for Heroes to die young, so his survival plan is:
stay anonymous, buy a collared fighting slave as a bodyguard-teacher, grow strong in
secret, free her the moment he can survive alone. He is hyper-scrupulous, morally
nauseated by what he's doing even as he judges it the least-bad option, and he is
falling in love and refusing to let himself act on it, because consent under a slave
collar is impossible. His baseline voice: controlled, precise, self-editing — a man
choosing every word from a shortlist of things he's allowed to say. His feelings run
hotter than anything he lets out. When his mask slips it slips toward grief, not anger.

**HER / HAURVATAT** (name never spoken until the tombstone; tag "Her"). Wolf-blood
beast-girl, sold young, collared. The collar *punished negative thoughts* — pain for
hating it, pain for despair, pain for remembering her own name — until she trained
herself never to think them. So her early-story cheer is NOT a lie she is knowingly
telling: it is conditioning, a smooth bright surface over a sealed room she herself
cannot open. What she genuinely feels early on is what the collar permits: eagerness to
be useful, comfort in routine, and one allowed flavor of distress — anxiety about *not
serving well*, about an unreadable master. Play the early cheer as real-but-thin;
frictionless; slightly too quick. The tell is flatness, never sadness. After the collar
comes off (ch11–12), the sealed room opens and twenty years of pain floods out; her real
voice underneath is older, drier, tired, precise, and genuinely fond of him. She is 23.
She dies by her own choice at the cliff, calm and resolved, and means her forgiveness.

**SKAGGANAUK, the Void Dragon.** Narrator; last boss of the last dungeon; older than the
cycle he describes. He has watched Hero and Demon Lord dance more times than anyone
counts, and he is telling this tale already knowing every wound in it. Feels: enormous
patience, detached compassion, a connoisseur's interest in change, and — at the very
end — something like hope. Never hurried, never loud. The register of a being for whom
this heartbreak is one of thousands, and who still bothered to tell it.

**BARTENDER.** Gruff, seen-it-all, genuinely rattled by Demon Lord talk (that fear is
real, not theater), otherwise enjoying himself at the kid's expense. His ch2 philosophy
lines are pub talk to him — he has no idea he's prophesying.

**Minor voices.** Announcer: a livestock auctioneer, professional relish, nothing
personal. Adventurers: beery, delighted gossips. Healer: dry, clinical, quietly on the
slave's side. Slavetakers: professionals first, sadists when crossed. Attendant: flat
temple-worker pity. Clerk: chirpy bureaucrat.

## Chapter 1 — Arrival (Skagganauk voiceover)

All chapter 1 lines are Skagganauk. One blanket state, with per-line shading: he is
settling into a tale he has grieved before. Measured, resonant, even. No urgency
anywhere; the reader's truck-death plays under his first line and he does not react to
it, because from where he sits it is a very old kind of event.

- **Skagganauk:** "I tell you now a tale of the planet, Elhom IV."
  - *feeling:* ceremonial calm; the pleasure of beginning. A door being opened slowly.
- **Skagganauk:** "The world of Elhom IV has a cycle."
  - *feeling:* patience of geological depth; "cycle" carries private weight — he has seen every turn of it.
- **Skagganauk:** "From time to time, arises a Demon Lord, feared and accursed."
  - *feeling:* detached irony, faint — he knows "feared and accursed" is the world's label, not his. (This line plays over Avram's arrival; the dragon knows exactly what he's implying. Do not wink. Let the words be flat and let the staging do the winking.)
- **Skagganauk:** "And another rises to fight them."
  - *feeling:* even, procedural; the second half of a law of nature.
- **Skagganauk:** "The great, the powerful, the Summoned Hero."
  - *feeling:* the epithets recited the way one recites a title one has heard too many times to be impressed by — respectful, unmoved.
- **Skagganauk:** "They are not supreme when they first arrive."
  - *feeling:* mild, almost fond amusement — watching a newcomer back away from a slime.
- **Skagganauk:** "But they fight, in forests, in arenas, in dungeon labyrinths."
  - *feeling:* a slow gathering of momentum; approval of effort, withheld judgment of outcome.
- **Skagganauk:** "And they gain in strength very quickly."
  - *feeling:* quiet emphasis; this is the load-bearing fact of the whole tale. Not ominous — *certain*.
- **Skagganauk:** "Sometimes, the outsiders are summoned from lands that tell stories of places like Elhom IV. It leaves less to explain."
  - *feeling:* dry humor, one degree above zero; the closest he comes to a joke in chapter 1.
- **Skagganauk:** "There is much about Elhom IV that Avram Detwiler finds strangely familiar."
  - *feeling:* the first time he says the boy's name; a specimen coming into focus. Interest sharpening, warmth still rationed.
- **Skagganauk:** "But what Avram has not been told, it seems,"
  - *feeling:* the storyteller's held breath — he is placing the hook deliberately. Feels the weight of what's coming.
- **Skagganauk:** "Is that there are always two outsiders Summoned, not one."
  - *feeling:* gravity, fully unsheathed for one line. This is the tale's engine and he knows it. Then let it go.

## Chapter 2 — Bartender

- **Avram:** "Glass of traveler's mead."
  - *feeling:* road-tired; alert underneath. He is not here to drink, he is here to fish. Low-level vigilance he's had since he arrived on this planet.
  - *presenting:* an unremarkable customer. Flat, easy, forgettable — forgettable is the goal.
- **Bartender:** "Eight copper."
  - *feeling:* zero interest. Another kid off the road.
- **Avram:** "Got a rumor I'd like to check on."
  - *feeling:* here it comes — a small tightening as he opens the real business. Casualness that took rehearsal.
  - *presenting:* idle curiosity, nothing riding on it.
- **Bartender:** "Twelve silver."
  - *feeling:* amused opportunism. Information costs extra, kid. A joke that's also a real price.
- **Avram:** "Not that kind of rumor - look, I'm not sure there's anything to this. Could just have been a guy messing with another guy. | But have you heard anything about a rising Demon Lord?"
  - *feeling:* he knows precisely how true the rumor is (he heard it from the entity that summoned him). The hedging is armor; the question itself he cares about intensely — he needs to know how far the news has spread and how much time he has. Tense across the shoulders, voice kept loose.
  - *presenting:* a man half-embarrassed to even ask; ready to laugh it off.
- **Bartender:** "Aw *slimespit.*"
  - *feeling:* gut-drop. Real dread, arriving faster than his manners.
- **Bartender:** "What'd you hear, kid? Tell me everything. Now."
  - *feeling:* frightened, and converting fright to aggression the way big men do. This is not a bit; if there's a Demon Lord he needs to know which continent to be off of.
  - *presenting:* intimidation — leaning on the kid because leaning is faster than asking nicely.
- **Avram:** "I just heard some guy talking about it! That's why I'm asking whether -"
  - *feeling:* startled by the sudden menace — genuine spike of alarm — plus a colder fear underneath: *do not let him find out how you actually know.*
  - *presenting:* an innocent bystander, hands metaphorically up.
- **Bartender:** "Some guy *where* saying *what?*"
  - *feeling:* still scared, pressing hard; interrogation reflex.
- **Avram:** "Some guy in the middle of nowhere who I heard say something about a Demon Lord! That's all I heard, okay? | I know practically nothing. That's why I'm *asking.*"
  - *feeling:* the exasperation is performed but the stress is real — he's improvising a cover story under pressure and it's technically-not-a-lie, which matters to him. Heart rate up; wording chosen on the fly with great care.
  - *presenting:* fed-up innocence; a guy regretting he brought it up.
- **Avram:** "If the Demon Lord has shown up, I'd really like to know where they are, what they're doing."
  - *feeling:* dead serious. This is the actual intelligence requirement, stated plainly because plain is safest.
  - *presenting:* reasonable-traveler prudence.
- **Avram:** "Whether their forces are invading here tomorrow."
  - *feeling:* same; the scenarios in his head are concrete.
  - *presenting:* slightly wry, keeping it hypothetical-sounding.
- **Avram:** "That kinda thing."
  - *feeling:* deliberately deflating his own question — cool-down move, lowering the temperature he raised. Watchful.
  - *presenting:* shrugging casualness.
- **Bartender:** "Deathstorms and balefire! No, I haven't heard anything."
  - *feeling:* relief with the adrenaline still draining — the oath is the fear leaving his body.
- **Bartender:** "I wouldn't be staying on the same continent if I'd heard the next Demon Lord had shown up here, ya know?"
  - *feeling:* candid; settling back down; a man explaining his actual survival policy.
- **Bartender:** "Or the Summoned Hero either, for that matter. | I'd wish the Hero the best of luck fighting, but not to the point where I'd want to die for gawking at it, ya know?"
  - *feeling:* comfortable cynicism; pleased with his own good sense. (For Avram, listening, this line is data: *civilians flee Heroes too.*)
- **Avram:** "Yeah, I know."
  - *feeling:* quietly filing that away; a small, private heaviness — the Hero this man would run from is standing in front of him buying mead.
  - *presenting:* idle agreement, half-attention.
- **Avram:** "Probably a dumb question, but if a guy runs across an unusual drop, is there any more profitable and sophisticated thing to do with it than flipping it to the Guild buyers?"
  - *feeling:* purposeful — he's costing out the auction purse. "Probably a dumb question" is deliberate self-deprecation to make the probing look naive. Mild embarrassment worn on purpose, like camouflage.
  - *presenting:* a greenhorn asking a money question.
- **Bartender:** "Guess what, kid? | You're right. | That is a dumb question."
  - *feeling:* enjoying himself now, fear fully forgotten; the pleasure of dunking on a rube. Each beat savored.
- **Bartender:** "Better hope nobody overheard it."
  - *feeling:* half-serious warning under the mockery — kids flashing wealth-talk get followed home. (Foreshadows the slavetakers' "guild source." Play it throwaway.)
- **Avram:** "You know, maybe this is another stupid thought. Feel free to tell me why it's stupid."
  - *feeling:* this is the important one. He is about to float *his actual life strategy* to a native and see if it survives contact. Genuinely wants it shot down if it's wrong. Focused; the "stupid" framing is protective coloration again.
  - *presenting:* bar-stool philosophizing.
- **Avram:** "But if Summoned Heroes are supposed to learn so fast, why don't they just stay hidden and keep leveling to the point where they can take out the Demon Lord quickly and without wrecking a continent?"
  - *feeling:* his creed, laid out as a hypothetical. Under the casual delivery: total conviction, and a live fear that there's a flaw in it he can't see.
  - *presenting:* idle what-if.
- **Avram:** "Like, geez, have some consideration for everybody who lives there."
  - *feeling:* the moral core said out loud, disguised as a quip. He means every word.
  - *presenting:* jokey exasperation.
- **Bartender:** "Ha! Doesn't sound very *heroic*."
  - *feeling:* genuine amusement; the concept strikes him as obviously wrong the way a fish finds bicycles wrong.
- **Avram:** "That's the only reason you can think of for why the Summoned Hero doesn't do that? It's not heroic enough?"
  - *feeling:* pressing — he needs a *real* objection, and "optics" isn't one. A note of relief creeping in (if that's the best counterargument, his plan stands), held in check.
  - *presenting:* debate-club needling.
- **Bartender:** "I don't think people become heroes without pride, ya know?"
  - *feeling:* comfortable folk wisdom; not defensive, just certain.
- **Avram:** "One might argue that the Summoned Hero ought to take pride in carefully executing their responsibility, to everyone in the world, to defeat the Demon Lord with minimum casualties and risk of failure."
  - *feeling:* his actual ethics, near-verbatim from his soul, hidden behind "one might argue." Steady, quietly passionate under the formal wrapper.
  - *presenting:* detached hypothetical, third person, nothing personal.
- **Bartender:** "Sounds more like a villain's style to me."
  - *feeling:* cheerful, offhand. He will never know what he just said. Do NOT lean on the line; its weight belongs entirely to Avram's silence later.
- **Bartender:** "If you think like that, the Summoned Hero ought to buy a high-level fighting slave and get carried through dungeons, like a sheltered noble scion, right? To avoid the risk that they get killed while they're still weak?"
  - *feeling:* riffing, building the absurd hypothetical for fun — and describing, exactly, what Avram is about to do. Play it as pure pub-logic momentum, mildly pleased with itself.
- **Bartender:** "But people like that aren't *heroes.* They'd never work up the courage to face the Demon Lord in the end."
  - *feeling:* the satisfied QED of a man winning an argument nobody was having. Warm, dismissive, final.
- **Avram:** "..." *(silent panel)*
  - *feeling:* chilled to the bone. The bartender has just described his entire plan and filed it under "villain," "coward," "not a hero." And he is going to do it anyway. The silence is him swallowing that — resolve hardening around a new splinter. If breath is recorded: one slow exhale, nothing more.

## Chapter 4 — Meeting

Walking away from the auction. He has just bought a person; the pamphlet is in his
pocket; he is close to vomiting and has a speech he rehearsed all week. She is walking
behind her fourth or fifth master, reading him for danger signs, finding none of the
usual ones, which is itself unsettling.

- **Avram:** "So. Um. I bought you to help me clear dungeons. And help teach me to fight. | I'm not planning to sexually assault you."
  - *feeling:* mortified, morally seasick, running on rehearsal. The rehearsed speech is already going wrong in his mouth — "sexually assault you" was supposed to sound reassuring and lands like a dropped plate. High tension: throat, jaw.
  - *presenting:* calm, businesslike decency; an employer stating terms.
- **Avram:** "You didn't *look* like the prospect of risking your life in a dungeon would horrify you to the point where you'd rather have gone with the other guy instead."
  - *feeling:* justifying the purchase to the person he purchased — and to himself, which is the harder audience. Slightly too fast; the arithmetic of the auction replaying behind his eyes.
  - *presenting:* reasoned, transparent decision-making.
- **Avram:** "It *looked* like you'd be better off with me. Like your life would be - better for my buying you, than my not buying you, if those were your only two options."
  - *feeling:* this is the exact moral ledger he's been living on since he raised his hand. Saying it aloud to check it holds. The dash before "better" is him flinching at his own accounting.
  - *presenting:* careful fairness.
- **Avram:** "If I was wrong about that - please speak up."
  - *feeling:* he means it completely, and he is afraid of the answer. A small, exposed pause of a line. Braced.
- **Her:** "Helping you clear dungeons is fine, Master! | Is there a reason you wouldn't also have sex with me?"
  - *feeling:* relief at a legible assignment — dungeons she understands. Then genuine confusion: the anomaly in his terms is a loose thread, and loose threads in a master are *dangerous*; she pulls it immediately, brightly. No shyness anywhere — the question is logistics to her.
  - *presenting:* bright, chipper, wholly at ease. (The presentation is conditioning; see cast notes. It costs her nothing here — it is her resting state.)
- **Avram:** "It's - against the customs of my people."
  - *feeling:* cornered; grabbing the prepared cover story like a handrail. Discomfort spiking — the question hit the exact thing he can't explain.
  - *presenting:* mild cultural fact, nothing to see here.
- **Avram:** "Are you hungry? Do you need a shower, or a doctor, or new underwear, or - or anything at all?"
  - *feeling:* fleeing the last topic into caretaking; guilt discharging as an over-long checklist, slightly frantic by the second "or." He needs to *do something kind immediately.*
  - *presenting:* practical logistics.
- **Her:** "No, Master. | I was fed just yesterday."
  - *feeling:* reporting good maintenance status — in her frame this is a *reassuring* answer, evidence she's low-cost. Zero awareness of how it lands. Content.
  - *presenting:* cheerful adequacy.
- **Avram:** "God. | How the hell do I say this? Do this?"
  - *feeling:* "fed just yesterday" went in like a knife. Momentary loss of composure — this is him talking to himself, to Earth, to God, not to her. Quiet, winded.
- **Avram:** "Um."
  - *feeling:* regathering. The sound of a man re-drafting a paragraph mid-air.
- **Avram:** "I want you to have an experience that is... as comfortable as it can be under these circumstances."
  - *feeling:* choosing words like stepping stones over ice water. "Under these circumstances" is doing enormous work — he can't say *slavery* out loud. Careful, strained.
  - *presenting:* considered, gentle policy-setting.
- **Avram:** "I don't mean that I'm ordering you to pretend to be happy."
  - *feeling:* horror at the failure mode he just imagined; pre-empting it urgently. He has thought about the ways kindness-by-command goes wrong.
- **Avram:** "Just... please let me know, how we can do this, in a way that minimizes fear and uncertainty for you. | On the inside."
  - *feeling:* the real request, and he knows how strange it must sound to her. "On the inside" said softer — he is pointing at the exact place the collar has sealed off, without knowing it.
- **Avram:** "A smile on the outside isn't what I'm looking for, not unless you mean it."
  - *feeling:* he has already clocked the auction-dais smile that looked fixed. Gently, deliberately placed. A key he is offering to a lock he can't see.
- **Avram:** "And the *point* of this whole concept is to make things easier on you, which means, I'm *not* going to punish you if you don't get it right."
  - *feeling:* trying to make the rule airtight, lawyer-against-himself; the emphasis is anxiety, not sternness — he's stressing the words because he's afraid they won't be believed.
  - *presenting:* patient clarity.
- **Her:** "Master's wishes... are my command? | I'm not sure I understand Master's wishes, though."
  - *feeling:* genuinely at sea. The bright mask lowers a notch (matter-of-fact) because puzzlement is *permitted*. Underneath: the one distress the collar allows — *I cannot tell what serving this man correctly looks like* — a low, real hum of anxiety.
  - *presenting:* dutiful willingness, slightly puzzled.
- **Avram:** "That's fine."
  - *feeling:* softening; relief that she said an honest thing.
- **Avram:** "Next we're going to the finest restaurant in this town and eating a nice meal together, including any of your favorite foods that are on the menu regardless of what they cost."
  - *feeling:* relief at reaching something concretely, unambiguously kind that he can actually *do*. First line of his that carries any lightness. Warmth allowed out, rationed.
- **Avram:** "And, if it's okay with you, I'd rather you didn't say anything about how grateful you are for such kindly treatment from me."
  - *feeling:* pre-flinching. Gratitude from a person he owns would burn. Awkward, honest.
  - *presenting:* a mild, odd request, apologetically made.
- **Her:** "Why not, Master? It's sort of an obvious thing to say."
  - *feeling:* honest puzzlement; social physics being contradicted. Not challenging — cataloguing another anomaly.
- **Avram:** "I mean, I *wish* I could say, it's okay for you to thank me if you want to. | But I won't actually be able to avoid looking horribly uncomfortable if you do."
  - *feeling:* surrendering to honesty about his own limits — the first fully-true personal thing he tells her. Rueful, small, almost funny. The closest thing to an actual human moment they've had.
  - *presenting:* (mask mostly down for this one line.)

*(Naming beat, same chapter — town gate.)*

- **Avram:** "So. What do I call you? What's your —"
  - *feeling:* trying for normalcy; light, hopeful — a getting-to-know-you question. He thinks this is the easy part of the day.
- **Her:** "Whatever Master likes! A slave's name is her master's to give."
  - *feeling:* **the only time in the whole story she interrupts him.** Under the brightness: a spike of pure trained danger-avoidance — his sentence was headed somewhere the collar tortured her over ("never to think of my old name"), and the conditioning slams the door before the question can finish existing. She does not consciously feel the pain; she feels only *urgency to be helpful right now.* The speed IS the scar.
  - *presenting:* extra-bright, eager helpfulness — brighter than baseline. Too fast.
- **Avram:** "No, I meant, the name you had before you —"
  - *feeling:* gently persisting; hasn't caught on yet. Kind, a little confused by the deflection.
- **Her:** "This says my name is #3,907,825. That's a lot of syllables for a dungeon, Master. You should pick something shorter."
  - *feeling:* skating fast away from the forbidden ground, converting it to practicality and wit before it can catch her. The joke is a *fire exit*. Tapping the collar keeps her hands busy.
  - *presenting:* bright, clever, tail-up; making it easy and funny.
- **Avram:** "..." *(silent panel — long sideways look)*
  - *feeling:* the first cold intimation that something is deeply wrong under the cheer. He can't name it yet. Watching her.
- **Avram:** "...I'll think on it."
  - *feeling:* deciding — consciously — not to push. Quiet; gentle; the sound of a man filing away a dread for later. Tension: low volume, high care.

## Chapter 5 — Teaching

*(Bedroll scene, campfire at night.)*

- **Her:** "Master. You look cold."
  - *feeling:* practical observation plus wanting-to-be-useful; also this is a service she *understands* — offering it puts the world back in a legible shape. Comfortable.
- **Avram:** "I'll live."
  - *feeling:* guard snapping up. Clipped because the alternative is a longer sentence he doesn't trust himself to say well. Cold, tired, wary of the whole topic.
  - *presenting:* stoic indifference.
- **Her:** "Master, part of the reason your bedroll is cold is that it was made to hold two people. It's not meant for you to be alone."
  - *feeling:* patient, factual persistence — she is *right about the bedroll* and knows it. A trace of the permitted worry: a master sleeping badly is a master served badly.
- **Avram:** "..." *(silent panel)*
  - *feeling:* the argument is annoyingly sound and he's freezing. Weighing principle against hypothermia.
- **Avram:** "...Fine. Warmth only."
  - *feeling:* conceding the small point while bolting every other door. "Warmth only" is a treaty line, drawn tense and precise. Defeated and rigid at once.
- **Her:** "Master, since we're sharing warmth anyway...?"
  - *feeling:* hopeful, testing — from inside her frame this is thrift (we're already here) plus the standing anxiety that unused services mean she isn't good enough. Light, genuinely without heat.
- **Avram:** "No."
  - *feeling:* immediate, total, and costing him more than the flatness admits — she is warm against his side and he is staring at the sky like a plank. The 'no' is to himself as much as her. Flat, gentle, absolutely closed.

*(Guild hall.)*

- **Clerk:** "Floor nine already?"
  - *feeling:* bureaucratic day brightened by a surprising number; friendly astonishment.
- **Avram:** "We've been lucky."
  - *feeling:* wariness — the number is *anomalous* and anomalies get noticed. Deflecting with modesty; mild wry awareness that "lucky" is a lie of the technically-true kind.
  - *presenting:* aw-shucks modesty.

## Chapter 6 — Tavern

- **Adventurer 1:** "Oi. That your slave?"
  - *feeling:* beery mischief; entertainment located. No malice yet, just sport.
- **Avram:** "She's with me."
  - *feeling:* the word "slave" lands wrong every time he hears it; the reframe is reflexive conscience. Guarded, level — he already smells where this is going.
  - *presenting:* neutral, don't-care.
- **Adventurer 1:** "Heard about you two. The boy who buys a beauty like that and bunks like a monk."
  - *feeling:* delight; local legend confirmed in the flesh. Playing to his table.
- **Adventurer 2:** "Maybe his sword arm's the only arm that works!"
  - *feeling:* triumphant with his own joke before he's finished saying it.
- **Adventurer 1:** "It's cruelty is what it is. Girl like that, and he makes her sleep *cold*."
  - *feeling:* mock-solemn — a lawyer for the prosecution, twinkling. The fun is in the deadpan.
- **Her:** "I don't sleep cold. Master shares the bedroll."
  - *feeling:* correcting a factual error about her master's care, brightly, mouth half full, priorities firmly on the skewer. The innuendo does not exist in her universe. Zero self-consciousness — that's the comedy and it must be played completely straight.
- **Adventurer 2:** "Shares the bedroll. And *nothing?*"
  - *feeling:* genuine awe; worldview damage.
- **Adventurer 1:** "That's not a monk, that's a *saint*. Or a corpse."
  - *feeling:* the punchline of a story he'll tell for years. Reverent mockery.
- **Avram:** "We're done."
  - *feeling:* alarm dressed as irritation — the table just broadcast his anomaly to a crowded room. Also plain embarrassment, which he resents having. Tight, flat, movement-in-the-voice (he's already standing).
  - *presenting:* bored finality.
- **Her:** "Goodnight, sers!"
  - *feeling:* cheerful sign-off, grabbing one last skewer; pleasant evening, nice men, good food. Weightless.

*(Outside the gate, night.)*

- **Her:** "Did I say something wrong, Master?"
  - *feeling:* the permitted distress, fully engaged: error-detection anxiety. Real worry, quiet and flat — this is what her fear is allowed to sound like: procedural.
- **Avram:** "No."
